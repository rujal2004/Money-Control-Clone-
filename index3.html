Account Aggregator FAQs

1. What is an Account Aggregator?

An Account Aggregator is a financial service platform that consolidates financial information from various sources like banks, insurance companies, investment firms, and other financial institutions approved by RBI, Sahamati. It provides a unified view of a user's financial data, making it easier to manage and analyze financial assets and liabilities. Currently you can track bank accounts, other investments are expected to come soon.

2. How do Account Aggregators ensure the security of my financial data?

Account Aggregators implement security measures such as encryption, secure authentication, and other industry-standard security protocols to ensure the safety and confidentiality of your financial data. They are regulated by financial authorities which mandate strict security and privacy measures.

3. Why use an Account Aggregator?

The use of account aggregators enables users to better track their bank accounts from providing users with comprehensive insights into their financial statuses to transactions conducted and bank balances of multiple accounts.

4. What are the benefits of using an Account Aggregator through Moneycontrol Platform?

5. How real-time are the updates provided by Account Aggregators?

The real-time nature of updates provided by Account Aggregators can vary. However, most offer near real-time or daily updates on account balances, transactions, and other financial activities, ensuring you have up-to-date information for decision-making.

9. Why isn't my account data refreshing?

If your account data isn't refreshing on the Account Aggregator platform, it could be due to various reasons. Common issues include: Limit of 4 refreshes after which your refresh is exhausted Data refreshes happen in regular intervals only Server downtime at your bank or financial institution Errors in account linking or incorrect login credentials

10. Can I manage multiple financial accounts with an Account Aggregator?

Yes, Account Aggregators are designed to manage multiple financial accounts. They collect and organize data from different financial institutions, providing a centralized platform to view and manage all your financial information.

11. How do Account Aggregators handle consent for data sharing?

Account Aggregators operate on a consent-based data sharing model. They require explicit consent from users before collecting or sharing financial information.

12. Can users revoke consent to share data?

Yes, All consent provided is designed to be revocable.

Yes, Account Aggregators are approved by RBI, Sahamati and are regulated entities.

15. Would my data be shared automatically if I join an Account Aggregator platform?

No, your consent would be required before your data is shared even after you have joined the Account Aggregator platform.

17. If I refresh a single bank account do all my accounts get refreshed?

All the bank accounts for which consent has been provided will be refreshed even if one bank account is refreshed.
